ARP spoofing occurs when an attacker sends fake ARP responses to associate their MAC address with the IP address of another device on the network. This allows the attacker to intercept or manipulate traffic between devices. To mitigate ARP spoofing attacks, network administrators can use static ARP entries for critical devices, enable Dynamic ARP Inspection (DAI) on managed switches, and segment networks using VLANs. Additionally, using encrypted protocols such as HTTPS and SSH ensures that even if traffic is intercepted, the data cannot be read by the attacker.
